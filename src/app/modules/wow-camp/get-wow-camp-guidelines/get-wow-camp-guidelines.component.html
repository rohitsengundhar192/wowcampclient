A typical student generates 10 to 15 kg of waste paper in a year. An average household generates about 30 kg of waste papers, used clothes and recyclable plastics (collectively referred to as WOW Waste ) per year.
Unlike traditional classrooms, the natural environment of an outdoor educational camp can improve physical fitness, mental well-being, and foster creativity, critical thinking, and provide a break from the distractions of technology and daily life for students. Outdoor leadership camps provide students with opportunities to develop important life skills such as teamwork, communication, problem-solving, and decision-making. Through the WOW (Well Being of Waste) Program, participating institutions can engage their students in certified out-of-classroom learning experiences by utilizing any of the natural scenic educational camps in the country that are adjacent to forests, rivers, lakes, or oceans and are accessible from a highway. There will be several WOW Volunteer-led team-building, leadership, outdoor, fun, and educational activities for the students to participate in and learn from. Typically, if a student is able to collect over 10 kgs of WOW Waste, he or she is eligible for 1 no. of WOW Camp Entry Ticket.
WOW, Volunteer - An authorized adult staff employed by the educational institution should accompany every 10 students to monitor their activities and the safety of the students throughout their camp trip and the entry for such personnel is free to the camp.
